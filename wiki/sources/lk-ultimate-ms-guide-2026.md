## Summary
Comprehensive market structure course (beginner to advanced). Most significant content: the rule that superseded structural levels become liquidity (not structural reference points), and the impulse vs correction framework for trade selection. Also addresses why trend lines fail and the supply/demand = range before impulse concept.

## Key Concepts Extracted

### Superseded Structural Levels Become Liquidity (CRITICAL)
> "The high that puts in a low is only important until that low has been broken — after which it becomes liquidity. Right? All that this means is that it's a future point of liquidity, meaning that at some point in the future price will revisit that price level but they don't mean anything structurally."

- Structural highs and lows serve a purpose *while the structure they created is intact*.
- Once a swing low is broken (CHoCH/BOS downward), the swing high that created it loses structural relevance.
- It becomes a **future liquidity target** — price will revisit it to collect stops clustered there.
- This is distinct from it being a current support/resistance level.
- **Implication for bot:** When a structural level is superseded, reclassify it from `structural_reference` to `liquidity_target`. It is now a draw, not a decision zone.

### Impulse vs Correction Framework
- **Impulsive move:** Aggressive, directional, multi-candle move in trend direction. This is where institutional orders execute.
- **Corrective move:** Choppy, counter-trend, internal structure. This is retail retracement. Do NOT trade corrections.
- **Rule:** Wait for price to complete correction and return to demand zone → enter on the NEXT impulse.
- Trying to trade within corrections = fighting the flow, high noise, low RR.

### Why Trend Lines Fail
- Trend lines are arbitrary — not based on actual swing points, drawn from subjective interpretation.
- By the time a trend line is visible and "obvious" to retail, it is often already late in the move.
- Market structure (objective swing highs/lows → HH/HL/LH/LL) is mechanical and unambiguous.
- Trend lines create predictable stop placement for institutions to sweep.
- **Rule:** Replace trend lines with swing structure identification. Never base entries on trend line support/resistance.

### Supply/Demand = Range Before Impulse
- Supply zone = ranging price (consolidation) BEFORE an aggressive bearish impulse.
- Demand zone = ranging price (consolidation) BEFORE an aggressive bullish impulse.
- This is the same as the OB concept at a higher level of abstraction.
- "Range expansion" = the aggressive impulse that follows the consolidation.

## Trading Rules Stated
- Only enter on impulsive moves in trend direction — never trade corrections.
- When a structural high/low is superseded, reclassify it as a liquidity target.
- Use market structure swing points (not trend lines) for all bias and entry decisions.
