## Summary
LK frames two root causes of losing trades — wrong bias and correct bias with wrong timing — and presents a 4-step London session strategy as the solution to both. The strategy anchors around the Asia session as a setup builder: Asia creates a range and the liquidity (Asia high/low) that London then sweeps. The sweep of Asia liquidity is the trigger event. LTF CHoCH from an OB provides the precise entry.

## Key Points
- Two root causes of all losing trades: (1) wrong directional bias, (2) correct bias but wrong timing.
- Both solved by the same structural foundation: HTF structure provides direction; LTF structure provides timing.
- Asia session (roughly 8pm–midnight EST) creates a high and low that contains a significant pool of resting liquidity.
- London session (2am–5am EST) commonly targets one side of the Asia range to sweep that liquidity.
- The bias tells you which side London will target; the sweep is the signal; the OB + CHoCH is the entry.
- TP1 at 5R (close 50%+ of position), TP2 at 15m swing high as the structural target.

## Trading Rules / Setups Described
1. **Step 1 — Time:** Trade London session only, specifically 2am–5am EST. This is when Asia liquidity is swept and institutional moves begin.
2. **Step 2 — Bias:** Determine directional bias from market structure. Are we bullish (targeting Asia low sweep → longs) or bearish (targeting Asia high sweep → shorts)?
3. **Step 3 — Asia Sweep Trigger:** For bullish bias: wait for price to trade below the Asia session low (sweeping lows, collecting stop losses). For bearish: wait for price to spike above the Asia high. Do NOT enter before the sweep — the sweep IS the setup trigger.
4. **Step 4 — LTF Confirmation:** After Asia low is swept, drop to 1m. Look for a bullish OB that price is now testing. Wait for 1m CHoCH bullish (1m bearish structure flips to bullish). Enter long from the OB level.
5. **Stop loss:** Below the 1m swing low established after the CHoCH.
6. **TP1:** 5R — close 50% or more of the position here.
7. **TP2:** 15m swing high (next structural target).
