## Summary
LK's comprehensive beginner guide — what he would do if starting over from scratch. The first lesson is "process over profits" — money is the result, not the goal; the process (following rules, managing risk, acting non-emotionally) is the cause. The farming analogy: a farmer doesn't dig up seeds daily to check progress — he focuses on daily process actions and trusts the harvest will come.

LK's backstory: started in crypto ~7–8 years ago, turned $15K into ~$100K in the 2017 bull run, then lost everything when market crashed. This formed the obsession that drove him to learn trading seriously.

No new technical trading rules. Pedagogically valuable for framing the entire LK educational approach.

## Key Points
- "Process over profits": money in trading is the effect, not the goal. Focus on the cause (process).
- Farmer analogy: daily process actions → harvest arrives. Trader who obsesses over profit = farmer who digs up seeds daily.
- LK's backstory: crypto 2017 bull run, $15K → $100K, then lost it all. That loss created the drive to learn.
- Beginners ask "How much money can I make today?" — professionals ask "Did I follow my rules today?"
- "Learning to learn" is a meta-skill: directed learning beats passive consumption.
- Beginners mistake: consume endless videos without a structured learning plan → feels productive but isn't.
