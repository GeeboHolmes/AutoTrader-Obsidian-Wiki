## Summary
LK presents a 5-phase smart money manipulation model that explains every major market move through a unified lens of institutional intent. This is the most conceptually complete explanation of WHY the market manipulates retail before moving — and provides a mechanistic entry/exit framework around the mitigation phase.

This model is highly compatible with LK's RLI framework (Range, Liquidation, Initiation from "lk-5-step-rli") and the Power of Three concept. It's the foundational institutional logic that underpins all LK strategies.

## Key Points
- **Phase 1 — Range:** Consolidation between a high and a low. Both BSL (above equal highs, resistance) and SSL (below equal lows, support) accumulate. Retail traders form biases (breakout traders, S/R traders) creating stop clusters on both sides.
- **Phase 2 — Liquidation:** Smart money sweeps one side (SSL in a bullish scenario) before the real move. Reason: they need sell-side liquidity (triggered stops = sell orders) to fill their buy orders at a favorable average price. This is a deliberate fake-out.
- **Phase 3 — Initiation:** After sweeping the liquidity, smart money shows their true hand. The key structural level (the high inside the range that triggered the liquidation) gets broken. This is the CHoCH/BOS that confirms institutional direction.
- **Phase 4 — Mitigation:** Price returns to the OB level (the sell-to-buy area where the manipulation occurred). Smart money closes short positions at break even AND fills more buy orders at that price. This is the actual entry point.
- **Phase 5 — Continuation:** After mitigation, price expands toward the pre-created liquidity on the opposite side (BSL). This is the profit-taking move.
- **Entry rule: Get in at Phase 4 (Mitigation). Exit at Phase 5 (Continuation target).**
- Asia session = Phase 1 (Range). London = Phase 2–3 (Liquidation + Initiation). NY = Phase 5 (Continuation).

## Trading Rules / Setups Described
1. **Identify Phase 1 (Range):** Asia session = the range. Mark Asia High and Asia Low.
2. **Identify Phase 2 (Liquidation):** London opens and sweeps Asia Low (if bullish) or Asia High (if bearish) with an aggressive move + rejection wick.
3. **Identify Phase 3 (Initiation):** After the liquidation, a key high gets broken (CHoCH). This is the confirmation that institutional direction is revealed.
4. **Identify Phase 4 (Mitigation — ENTRY ZONE):** The sell-to-buy OB level (last bearish candle before the aggressive buy impulse that caused the CHoCH). Wait for price to return to this level.
5. **Confirm entry:** Drop to 1m. Wait for 1m CHoCH at the mitigation zone (same principle — lower time frame must also show the shift).
6. **Enter long from mitigation zone OB.** Stop below the zone. Target: next BSL (Phase 5 target).

## Contradictions / Open Questions
- Mitigation phase (Phase 4) is described as price RETURNING to the OB. This is the retest of the manipulation zone. However, LK elsewhere states "don't enter at the OB directly — wait for 1m CHoCH." Those two rules are consistent — the mitigation is the zone you watch, and the 1m CHoCH is the trigger within that zone.
- "Closed their shorts at break even" — LK suggests institutional activity inside the mitigation zone involves closing hedge positions. This is theoretical and unverifiable, but the key observational truth is that price repeatedly reacts from these zones.
