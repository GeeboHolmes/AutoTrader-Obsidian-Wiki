## Summary
LK presents a 3-rule strategy with a unique structure: rather than dedicating a rule to entry location, the third rule is dedicated entirely to **trade management** — how to protect and extract maximum value from a winning position. LK shows a $35,000 trade (EUR/USD, 12 November), calling it live to his inner circle 7 hours before execution.

The distinguishing feature is LK's validated management system: TP1 at 5R (50% of position), TP2 at major session level, TP3 at swing high; 97% of break-even moves saved money across 1000+ trades of data. He frames a losing first trade as a positive — it placed him at a better (lower) entry for the second attempt, resulting in a higher RR on the winner.

## Key Points
- 3 rules: (1) directional bias, (2) sniper entries (LTF CHoCH), (3) trade management
- Rule 3 is management, not an entry condition — unique among LK strategies
- TP1: 5R, take 50% of position; move stop to break even
- TP2: major session level (HOD, equal highs, session high)
- TP3: external swing high (full target)
- 97% of break-even trades saved money in LK's 1000+ trade sample
- Asia low is mentioned as required ("buyer below Asia session low") — implicit rule 2.5
- Losing the first trade = better entry for second attempt = higher RR on winner

## Trading Rules / Setups Described
1. **Rule 1 — Directional Bias:** 15m external structure (box method, external swing points only). Bullish = HH/HL.
2. **Rule 2 — Sniper Entries (LTF CHoCH):** When 15m is bullish and price pulls back, 1m timeframe will be bearish. Wait for 1m to shift bullish (CHoCH). Only enter when 1m structure aligns with 15m direction. Stop at 1m swing low. This converts 1:3 entries into 1:13 by dramatically tightening stop.
3. **Rule 3 — Trade Management:**
   - On entry, target swing high but do NOT hold full position to final target
   - TP1: 5R — take 50% of position off; move stop to break even
   - TP2: major structural level (session high, equal highs, HOD) — take another portion
   - TP3: external swing high — close remainder
   - Move to break even after any initial push; 97% of BE moves saved money in sample data
