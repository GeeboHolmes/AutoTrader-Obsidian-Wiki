## Summary
LK's earliest dedicated OB selection video (June 2023). Addresses three common OB mistakes, then presents the five principles required for a valid, high-probability order block. Core thesis: "Spot the liquidity or BE the liquidity" — if your OB sits next to no liquidity that has already been swept, your OB IS the liquidity and price will use it.

The video walks through a live GBP/USD price leg applying all five principles simultaneously to demonstrate how to select valid OBs from the field of apparent OBs.

## Key Points
- The market has one objective: collect orders. If your OB is the nearest unswept order cluster, it will be consumed.
- An OB is only valid if it exists inside the current trading range — stale OBs from previous ranges are irrelevant.
- Imbalance (FVG between candle 1 and candle 3) is mandatory — it is the confirmation that institutional orders were placed there.
- The five principles must ALL be present — any missing principle degrades probability substantially.

## Trading Rules / Setups Described

**Three biggest mistakes (what NOT to do):**
1. Selecting OBs that have not swept any liquidity and have no liquidity to target — your OB is the liquidity.
2. Selecting OBs from stale trading ranges — if structure has shifted since the OB was formed, it is no longer relevant.
3. Selecting random buy-to-sell / sell-to-buy candles that have no structural significance — "supply and demand zones appear everywhere; the key is which ones matter."

**Five principles for a valid OB (ALL required):**
1. **OB must take liquidity OR have liquidity to take.** Either the OB candle itself already swept a liquidity level, OR there is a clear liquidity level (equal highs/lows, swing high/low) in the direction of the trade ahead of price.
2. **OB must break something significant.** The move from the OB must cause a BOS or CHoCH — it must be the OB that drives the structural break.
3. **OB must be relevant to current price action.** It must be inside the current trading range (low-to-high box of the present structural leg). OBs from previous legs are stale.
4. **OB must have imbalance.** Candle 1 high must NOT touch candle 3 low — a FVG must exist. This confirms institutional order flow at that candle.
5. **OB must have a liquidity pool to target.** When price returns to the OB and is filled, it needs liquidity above (for longs) or below (for shorts) to travel toward — equal highs/lows, trendline liquidity, structural highs/lows.
