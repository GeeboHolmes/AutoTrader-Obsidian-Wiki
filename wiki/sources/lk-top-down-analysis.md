## Summary
Lewis Kelly's most explicit breakdown of his three-timeframe top-down approach. Uses three live trades taken in one session to demonstrate the method. The core insight is that the daily candle's character (direction, position within day's range) drives intraday trade selection. Also introduces the "mean reversion" concept — when a daily candle is at an extreme (near the high or low), expect a snapback move toward the close, creating a secondary trade opportunity.

## Key Points
- **Three timeframes:** Daily → 15m → 1m (entry on 5m FVG/OB after 1m confirmation)
- **Daily candle = the master context:** Read the last 4–5 daily candles for macro bias. 4 consecutive bearish days = bearish continuation expected.
- **Daily FVG/IFVG:** Mark these on the daily chart as directional targets for the week
- **Mean reversion principle:** The daily candle's low is almost always lower than its close. After an aggressive bearish session, expect a bounce to occur before close. This is a secondary trade opportunity.
- **Sell when daily candle is bullish:** In a bearish trend, the daily counter-trend push (bullish candle) is the manipulation phase — sell into it. Enter when the 1m confirms reversal from the high of day.
- **Buy when daily candle is bearish (at extreme):** In a bearish trend, when the daily candle is far overextended below its open, expect a bounce — the low of day is in.
- **Two-candle pullback confirmation:** On the 1m, a 2-candle bearish pullback (for longs) that then gets broken to the upside = entry trigger (simpler version of the 3-candle rule).
- **Inverse FVG (IFVG):** A filled FVG that has flipped polarity. On the daily, this is a significant entry/target zone.

## Trading Rules / Setups Described
**LK Three-Timeframe Top-Down Process:**
1. **Daily:** Read the last 4–5 days. Determine macro bias (4 bearish days = bearish). Identify daily FVG/IFVG as weekly target. Note daily candle's current character (bullish or bearish within the session).
2. **15m:** New daily candle opens. Note London session open (2 a.m. EST). Confirm 15m structure aligns with daily bias. Identify any internal liquidity (Asia high/low) that needs to be swept first.
3. **1m:** When the daily candle is moving counter to bias (e.g. bullish daily candle during bearish trend) → look for 1m structure to reverse. Wait for 1m CHoCH from bullish to bearish.
4. **5m entry:** After 1m CHoCH, find 5m FVG or OB at the level. Enter. Stop loss above the high of day (for shorts) or below the low of day (for longs). Target: session liquidity opposite direction.

**Mean Reversion Trade:**
1. Daily candle at extreme (near low of day after aggressive bearish session).
2. Look for exhaustion signs: 15m failing to close below previous 15m candle low.
3. 1m: break of 2-candle bullish pullback = long entry trigger.
4. Entry from 5m/3m FVG. Stop below the low of day. Target: close of day range (1:3–1:5 RR typical).

## Contradictions / Open Questions
- LK mentions "Inverse Fair Value Gap (IFVG)" in this video. The IFVG is a filled FVG that has flipped polarity. This is slightly different from ICT's IFVG definition. Need to clarify and create a dedicated concept note.
- The "daily candle" as a bias driver is a simplification of traditional top-down analysis. Works well for intraday but may need weekly/monthly context for swing trading.
- Mean reversion trade: "low of day almost always lower than close" — testable claim. Worth backtesting on BTC/USDT to validate.
