## Summary
A detailed breakdown of a London short trade on GBP/USD that ran 19.5R (LK says he didn't hold for the full run). The primary educational value is the application of the complete top-down framework (Weekly → 4H → 15m → 1m) to a real trade, plus an important extension: using DXY (Dollar Index) correlation to confirm GBP/USD bias, and treating high-impact news events (NFP in this case) as engineered inducement before the real institutional move.

The video shows LK's pre-trade forecast in a live session, then walks through the full analysis. Multiple confluences stacked: higher timeframe bearish, 4H FVG, 4H demand OB, previous month's low as liquidity target, DXY alignment.

## Key Points
- **News events as manipulation:** Before major news (NFP), institutions engineer liquidity on the opposing side. Price moves aggressively in one direction during/after news, creating an FVG. That FVG becomes the primary rebalancing target. LK's rule: "Don't fight the direction that NFP revealed — that is the institutional intention."
- **Monthly highs/lows as liquidity:** Previous month high/low = always a key liquidity level. Stacks with 4H demand OB and FVG = highest confluence entry.
- **DXY inverse correlation for GBP/USD:** DXY bullish = GBP/USD bearish. When DXY structure aligns with GBP/USD structure at the same time frame (4H both making BOS), the trade bias is confirmed. Used as a secondary confirmation, not a primary entry trigger.
- **FVG as pullback target:** After an aggressive BOS that leaves a large FVG, price will almost certainly return to rebalance it before continuing. The FVG is the zone to watch for entry — specifically the OB within the FVG zone.
- **Liquidation ≠ BOS (confirmed again):** "Price liquidated the highs — didn't break — that's the key thing." Wick above a high with body close below = liquidation. Only body close above = BOS. This is consistent with earlier LK sources.
- **Strong vs targeted highs:** After CHoCH, the swing high from which the CHoCH originated is "strong" (will be protected). The newly created swing low is "targeted." This determines where the range is and what direction to trade.
- Trade: GBP/USD London short. Entry after 15m CHoCH to downside + price in 4H FVG/demand zone. 19.5R to the monthly low target.
- **Complex pullbacks create equal highs/lows:** When price makes a complex pullback (multiple legs), equal highs form. These signal where stop clusters are accumulating — a good thing as it increases the liquidity pool before the next expansion.

## Trading Rules / Setups Described
**Top-Down Framework Applied:**
1. Weekly/4H: Establish bearish trend (BOS + CHoCH sequence). Identify FVG from aggressive bearish expansion. Identify 4H demand OB inside FVG zone.
2. DXY confirmation: 4H DXY bullish → expect GBP/USD bearish. Both must align.
3. Mark monthly lows as primary liquidity target for the move.
4. 15m: After 4H demand OB is reached, observe 15m structure for CHoCH in bearish direction (realignment with 4H intent). Identify sell-to-buy OB at the CHoCH level.
5. 1m: Enter from 1m OB at the 15m structure. Stop above 1m high. Target monthly low (or FVG on the other side).
6. Pre-news: Sit out during NFP manipulation. After news, re-read structure to confirm institutional direction, then apply normal top-down analysis.
