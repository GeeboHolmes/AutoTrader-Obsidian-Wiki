## Summary
LK's 5-step framework for reaching consistent profitability ($10k/month via prop firms). Less about SMC mechanics and more about the complete professional trading pipeline: from technical competence through to psychology and performance review. The "Performance Gap" concept — the delta between theoretical backtest results and actual trading results — is introduced as the real bottleneck for most capable traders. A structured Trading Operating System (OS) is presented as the solution.

## Key Points
- $10k/month model: $200k prop firm account × 5% monthly return.
- 5 steps to profitability: (1) Technical analysis competence (one method, stick with it), (2) Edge (backtested trading plan), (3) Testing (100+ trades to validate statistics), (4) Performance Gap, (5) Operating System.
- 70% of aspiring traders fail at step 1, another 20% at step 2, 5% at step 3 — leaving only 5% even reaching the performance gap stage.
- Performance gap = gap between theoretical results (what the strategy could make) and actual results. Cause: psychology, inconsistent plan execution.
- "Alignment > Outcome": a profitable trade taken outside the plan is dangerous (trains the brain that breaking rules is rewarded). An aligned but losing trade is healthy.
- Trading OS = centralized system tracking every trade decision, outcome, and alignment score. Weekly review of alignment (not P&L) is the key metric.

## Trading Rules / Setups Described
_(framework-level, not entry rules)_
1. Pick one technical method (LK recommends SMC). Do not combine multiple systems.
2. Build a complete trading plan covering: asset selection, session/time, bias method, entry conditions, stop placement, TP rules, position sizing, break-even rules.
3. Backtest the plan on 1 year of historical data (all qualifying setups, day by day).
4. Identify inefficiencies in results; run hypotheses to improve. Iterate until statistics are acceptable.
5. Build a trading OS: spreadsheet or database tracking every trade with fields for: setup conditions, entry/exit, outcome, and crucially — alignment score (was this trade in plan?).
6. Review alignment weekly. Make decisions based on alignment trends, not short-term P&L.
