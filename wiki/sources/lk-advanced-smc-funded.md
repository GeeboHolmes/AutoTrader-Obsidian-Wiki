## Summary
LK presents an advanced 4-step strategy framed for prop firm / funded account traders (FTMO, etc.). The key advancement over beginner models is **stacked liquidity** — identifying when multiple types of liquidity pools coincide at the same price level (trendline + Asia high + equal highs + weak highs all at the same zone = extremely high probability). 

The market structure section is also more sophisticated: LK introduces the "internal realignment signal" — when price makes an internal CHoCH within a larger external range, it signals the range is about to break, allowing earlier entries. This source represents the most technically advanced treatment of LK's concepts.

## Key Points
- Primary audience: prop firm traders where consistency matters more than RR
- **Stacked liquidity**: multiple liquidity types coinciding = highest probability setups
- Stacked types: trendlines, Asia highs/lows, equal highs/lows, weak highs (last structural high before a LL), prior session extremes
- Market structure: 15m primary with 1H for large ranges — use whichever timeframe clearly shows external structure
- Internal realignment signal: internal CHoCH within external range → signals external BOS incoming
- Institutional area of interest: FVG + OB confluence required (single zone insufficient for advanced model)
- LTF CHoCH entry on 1m after confirming stacked liquidity and institutional zone
- Stop: above sweep wick high; target: next liquidity pool

## Trading Rules / Setups Described
1. **Step 1 — Market Structure (15m / 1H):** Use 15m for most pairs; use 1H when market is in a wide range. External structure only. Wick = liquidation event (not a BOS). Internal CHoCH within a larger external range = "internal realignment" signal — price is about to deliver to the opposite external extreme. This provides early warning for the next external BOS direction.
2. **Step 2 — Stacked Liquidity Identification:** Scan for a price level where multiple liquidity types coincide. Minimum two types for a valid setup; three or more = A+ setup. Types to stack: (a) trendline with 3+ touches, (b) Asia session high or low, (c) equal highs or equal lows (two+ touches at same level), (d) weak high (last swing high that formed before a lower low), (e) prior session high/low. Mark the stacked zone as the target liquidity pool.
3. **Step 3 — Institutional Area of Interest:** After identifying the stacked liquidity zone, identify where price will likely retrace to before the expansion. Require FVG + OB confluence (both must be present). Single-type zones insufficient for this model. The institutional zone is the entry zone.
4. **Step 4 — LTF CHoCH Entry:** When price enters the institutional zone (Step 3), drop to 1m. Wait for 1m CHoCH in the direction of the trade (toward the stacked liquidity pool). Enter on the CHoCH candle or at the subsequent 1m OB. Stop above the entry zone high (shorts) or below entry zone low (longs). Target: the stacked liquidity pool identified in Step 2.
