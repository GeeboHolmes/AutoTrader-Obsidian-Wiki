## Key Points
- **Three phases of price:** Bullish expansion (HH/HL), consolidation (range-bound), bearish expansion (LH/LL). These three phases cycle continuously.
- **CHoCH definition (clearest to date):** CHoCH occurs when the "responsible low" is broken — the swing low that was specifically responsible for creating the last structural high. Breaking any other low is NOT a CHoCH; it is either noise or an internal structure event.
- **Internal vs external (reinforced):** The most common trader mistake is using internal structure lows/highs to determine bias. "If it's above this low, it's bullish. Until that external low breaks, everything else is noise."
- **Wicks = liquidation:** A wick through a level without a candle close is not a structural break. It is a liquidity sweep/liquidation. Only candle closes define BOS/CHoCH.
- **3-candle pullback rule:** A valid pullback requires a minimum of 3 consecutive candles moving against the trend. One or two candles = not a structural pullback.
- **Supply/demand zones:** Demand = the area of consolidation (ranging price action) immediately before an aggressive bullish move. The bulk of volume before the expansion = the demand zone.
- **Entry "cheat code":** 15m bullish structure + 15m demand zone + 1m CHoCH (bearish 1m structure shifts to bullish inside the demand zone) = triple-confirmation entry. Improves RR from ~1:2 to ~1:5+.

## Trading Rules / Setups Described
1. Identify HTF structure (4H or Daily) to determine bias.
2. Only use external swing points — the structural swing that was responsible for the last BOS. Ignore all internal highs/lows.
3. When price retraces into the structural range, locate the supply/demand zone (area of consolidation before the last aggressive expansion).
4. Drop to 1m. Observe bearish 1m price action as price enters the zone.
5. Wait for 1m bullish CHoCH (1m bearish structure breaks to bullish by taking out a 1m high).
6. Enter long at the 1m CHoCH level. Stop below 1m swing low.
7. Target: next structural high of the HTF leg.
