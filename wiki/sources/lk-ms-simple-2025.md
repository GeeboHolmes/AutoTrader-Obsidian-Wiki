## Summary
LK presents market structure as the single most important foundation of trading — specifically the need for a rule-based, objective system for determining directional bias rather than relying on discretionary judgment. Frames a live trade breakdown ($6,000 in a few hours) to illustrate how the rule-based structural system generates high-probability entries. Reinforces the external vs internal structure distinction and multiple-candle pullback requirement.

## Key Points
- Directional bias must be determined by a rule-based system, not by feeling or intuition. "My rules answer that question for me, not me."
- Only use external structural swing points. Anything that doesn't meet the criteria for a valid pullback (multi-candle) is internal noise, not structure.
- Fibonacci 50% line defines premium/discount — anything below 50% = discount (buy zone for bullish markets).
- Sell-to-buy OB identified as primary entry zone: the final bearish candle before the bullish expansion at the discount level.
- Liquidity sweep required before entry: equal lows or session lows must be swept below the OB before trading from it.
- Trade example: 4H bullish → 15m structure confirms pullback is internal → price in discount (below 50%) → sell-to-buy OB in discount + equal lows below → wait for sweep → 1m confirmation.

## Trading Rules / Setups Described
1. Determine 4H structural bias (HH/HL = bullish; LH/LL = bearish).
2. Apply premium/discount to the 4H structural leg: below 50% = discount for longs.
3. On 15m, identify the pullback phase — confirm it is using external swing points (multi-candle pullback).
4. Locate a sell-to-buy OB within the 15m discount zone.
5. Check for unswept liquidity (equal lows) below the OB. If present, wait for the liquidity sweep before entering.
6. After liquidity sweep, drop to 1m for entry confirmation (LTF CHoCH or OB within the discount).
7. Entry from OB level. Stop below the swept liquidity low. Target: 4H swing high or next structural high.
