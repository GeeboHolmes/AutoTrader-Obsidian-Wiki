## Summary
LK presents a 1-minute scalping strategy where the entry is specifically from the 1m OB (the "trading range" / accumulation zone) that caused the CHoCH, rather than from a 5m FVG. 1:7 RR shown live. Key addition: detailed break-even management — wait for price to form a pullback + continuation above the OB before moving to BE, not just a push away.

## Key Points
- Same 3-step framework: 15m bias + Asia sweep + 1m LTF CHoCH
- Entry distinction: enter from the 1m OB range (the candle/range cluster that caused the 1m CHoCH), not a 5m FVG
- Stop: below the 1m OB low (small stop — 4.5 pip example)
- Target: 15m structural high/low = 1:7 RR shown on EUR/USD
- BE management: after price pushes away from OB, wait for a pullback + continuation above the OB zone before moving SL to BE (not just any initial push)
- Logic: if price pulls back and the OB holds with a continuation candle, then it's confirmed the OB level is cleared and no reason for price to return

## Trading Rules / Setups Described
1. **Step 1 — 15m Intraday Bias:** External structure. HH/HL = bullish. LH/LL = bearish. Identify the current swing high and swing low.
2. **Step 2 — Asia Liquidation:** Asia session set. Wait for London (or NY) to run the Asia session low (for longs) or Asia high (for shorts). Sweep confirms the liquidity grab.
3. **Step 3 — 1m CHoCH + OB Entry:** After sweep, drop to 1m. Track most recent swing high. Wait for it to be broken (CHoCH to bullish for longs). Identify the 1m OB: the candle cluster / "trading range" where the CHoCH originated (the last bearish range before the bullish breakout). Enter at the open of this range. Stop: 4.5 pips below the range low.
4. **BE Management:** After entry, monitor 1m price action. When price pushes away from the OB range, do NOT move to BE immediately. Wait for: (1) price to pull back into the zone, (2) the zone to hold (no break below), (3) price to break the high of the pullback candle. Only then move SL to BE. Rationale: premature BE = gets stopped out on normal OB mitigation before continuation.
