## Summary
Four-part market structure progression: basic → advanced → multi-dimensional → trading model. Introduces "range expansion" as a core rhythm concept and uses the "piggyback from a whale" analogy for institutional alignment. Uses "chuck" as spoken abbreviation for CHoCH. Conceptually consistent with other LK MS content; primary value is the 4-part progression framework.

## Key Concepts Extracted

### Four-Part MS Mastery Model
1. **Basic:** HH/HL/LH/LL identification on a single timeframe. Directional bias only.
2. **Advanced:** Internal vs external structure. Recognising when internal moves are fake-outs vs genuine shifts.
3. **Multi-Dimensional:** Multiple timeframe alignment. HTF bias + ITF confirmation + LTF entry.
4. **Trading Model:** Combining structure with liquidity targets and entry tools (OBs/FVGs). Full trade workflow.

### Range Expansion = Supply/Demand Rhythm
- Market alternates between **ranging** (consolidation, balance) and **expanding** (impulse, institutional execution).
- "Range expansion, range expansion" = the underlying rhythm.
- Ranging phases = where institutions accumulate/distribute.
- Expansion phases = where price moves aggressively toward the next liquidity target.
- Consistent with the OB/demand zone concept and [[sources/lk-ultimate-ms-guide-2026]] supply/demand formulation.

### "Piggyback from a Whale" Analogy
- Retail traders cannot move markets; institutions can.
- The correct approach: identify where institutional orders are placed (OBs, demand zones) and enter in the same direction.
- "You piggyback from the whale" — trade with institutional flow, never against it.

### Terminology Note
- LK verbally abbreviates "CHoCH" as "chuck" throughout this video.
- Same concept: lower timeframe change of character / structure shift.
