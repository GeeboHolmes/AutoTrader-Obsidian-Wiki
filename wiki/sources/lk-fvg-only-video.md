## Summary
LK presents a definitive FVG guide focused on the institutional mechanics behind why FVGs exist and why they consistently provide reactions. The core thesis: FVGs are not just price "gaps" — they are areas of extremely low volume created when market makers legally withdraw liquidity, most commonly during high-impact news events. Volume profile is introduced as a validation tool to confirm genuine FVGs vs random gaps.

The video establishes a three-rule framework for trading FVGs, linking volume inefficiency, trend alignment, and liquidity context as the three pillars of a high-probability FVG trade.

## Key Points
- FVGs exist because market makers are legally allowed to withdraw liquidity during news events, causing price to move aggressively through areas with no counterparty transactions.
- A real FVG = extremely low volume area (confirmed by volume profile). A random gap with normal volume is not a high-probability FVG.
- Entry model: price sweeps into the FVG (rebalance), then a 5m CHoCH confirms institutional re-engagement before entry.
- FVG alone = not enough. Must combine with trend (MS) and liquidity context.
- Partial fill to the 50% midpoint of the FVG is often sufficient for a reaction — full fill is not required.

## Trading Rules / Setups Described
1. **Rule 1 — Volume Inefficiency:** Confirm the FVG contains genuinely low volume using a volume profile overlay. The gap zone should show a low-volume node (LVN). A gap with normal volume distribution is not a valid FVG.
2. **Rule 2 — Trend Alignment:** The FVG must be in the direction of the overall market trend (external structure). Never trade a bearish FVG in a bullish market.
3. **Rule 3 — Liquidity Context:** A liquidity level (equal highs/lows, session H/L) must have been swept into the FVG, and there must be a clear liquidity target ahead in the trade direction. FVG = entry; liquidity target = take profit.
4. **Entry trigger:** Once price enters the FVG zone, drop to 5m and wait for 5m CHoCH in trade direction before executing.
5. **Stop loss:** Below the FVG low (for bullish FVG) with a small buffer.
6. **Take profit:** Next liquidity level or structural high/low in the trend direction.
