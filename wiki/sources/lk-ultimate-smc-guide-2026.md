## Summary
Lewis Kelly's most comprehensive recent SMC overview, covering supply and demand, market structure, liquidity, and a complete 4-step trading strategy. The video argues that most traders fail because they over-complicate SMC — the profitable edge comes from combining just three concepts (institutional supply/demand, market structure, liquidity) into a repeatable system. The strategy is demonstrated on live executed trades across multiple sessions.

## Key Points
- **Only three concepts matter:** Supply & demand (OBs), market structure (bias), liquidity (context)
- **Order blocks** = the range before an aggressive expansion; institutional orders load in the range, price returns to fill them
- **Internal vs external structure** is the most critical and most misunderstood distinction in SMC
- **"If you're buying before liquidity is taken, you ARE the liquidity"** — wait for the sweep before entering
- **Liquidity to take (LTT) + Liquidity to target (LTT²)** = the dual liquidity framework every trade needs
- Session liquidity (Asia/London/NY highs-lows) and previous day H/L are the most reliable liquidity levels
- **Market structure** = external swing points only; internal structure is noise until the external range breaks

## Trading Rules / Setups Described
1. **Step 1 — Market structure bias:** Use 15m chart. Mark external swing high and swing low. Determine bullish (HH/HL) or bearish (LH/LL) bias. This is the filter — trade only with the bias.
2. **Step 2 — Institutional demand/supply:** Mark the range-before-expansion (OB) in the direction of bias. Both the full consolidation range and the micro range + expansion within it are valid OBs.
3. **Step 3 — Cleared liquidity + liquidity to target:** Before entering, confirm: (a) the sell-side liquidity below has been swept (longs) or buy-side swept (shorts); (b) there is remaining liquidity above (for longs) to target. If no liquidity to target, no trade.
4. **Step 4 — LTF confirmation:** When price enters the OB, switch to 1m chart. Wait for 1m market structure to shift in the direction of the trade (bearish → bullish for longs). This is the entry trigger.
5. **Entry:** After 1m CHoCH, switch back to 15m or 5m, find the range-before-expansion at the entry level, place entry, stop below OB low, targets at session/day liquidity levels.

## Contradictions / Open Questions
- Lewis Kelly uses the 15m as his primary bias timeframe. This differs from many ICT setups that start at 4H or Daily for bias. Both approaches are valid but produce different setups — note which framework a strategy page is using.
- LK marks OBs as the "range" (entire consolidation) before expansion, not strictly the "last opposing candle" as in pure ICT. This is a wider zone but easier to identify programmatically.
