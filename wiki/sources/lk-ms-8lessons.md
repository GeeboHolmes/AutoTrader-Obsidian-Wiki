## Summary
An 8-lesson comprehensive market structure course. The standout new content (Lesson 4) defines **weak vs strong structure** — a concept not explicitly named in earlier LK videos. This is a critical framework for understanding which swing points are likely to be swept (targeted as liquidity) vs which are protected (institutional support/resistance).

Other lessons cover external vs internal structure, premium/discount with Fibonacci, and how to combine all elements into a complete strategy with a step-by-step trade breakdown.

## Key Points
- **External vs internal structure:** External swing points (the most recent significant high and low) define the actual range. Everything inside is internal. Traders who misread internal structure as the actual trend are the majority of losers.
- **Box method confirmed:** Draw box from swing low to swing high. Ignore all internal action. Await external breakout.
- **Premium/discount:** Same Fibonacci approach — buy in discount (below 50%), sell in premium (above 50%). 61.8–78.6% = golden zone. Applies to every price leg.

**Lesson 4 — Weak vs Strong Structure (CRITICAL):**
- A **structural swing point** has a "job" — a high's job is to break the corresponding low; a low's job is to break the corresponding high.
- If a high **succeeds** in breaking the corresponding low → that low becomes **strong** (protected by the institution that just broke it; they don't want price above their entry).
- If a high **fails** to break the corresponding low (starts reversing before doing so) → that high is **weak** (cannot be protected; will be targeted as liquidity).
- **Practical application:** When you see a high that never managed to break the most recent structural low, and price starts reversing back up toward it, that high is WEAK → it will be swept. Mark it as a liquidity target, not a resistance level.
- **Multiple failures = increasing weakness:** Each time a high tries and fails to take the corresponding low, it becomes more confirmed as a weak/targeted level.
- Strong lows (that have successfully broken highs above) will be defended by institutions — these are the "protected" lows from which you can buy.

## Trading Rules / Setups Described
**Lesson 1 — Basic identification:** BOS = external swing break. CHoCH = first break in opposite direction.
**Lesson 2 — Reality of price delivery:** Internal structure = pullbacks only. Never trade internal breaks as if they are structural reversals.
**Lesson 3 — Premium/discount:** Fib from leg high to leg low. Buy below 50%, sell above 50%. Optimal: 61.8–78.6% retracement.
**Lesson 4 — Weak/strong structure:** Identify weak highs (failed to take the low below) as future liquidity targets. Strong lows (have taken a high) as institutional entry support.
**Lesson 5–8 (implied):** Combine external structure + premium/discount + weak/strong structure + OB confirmation for full strategy execution.
