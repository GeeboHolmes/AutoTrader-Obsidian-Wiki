## Summary
LK presents the single concept that reduced his losing trades: candle closure confirmation. The core rule: a wick through a high or low is NOT a BOS — price must CLOSE above/below the level to confirm structure. Deep order book explanation of why: a wick simply triggers stop-losses / pending orders at that level, but if price cannot maintain value above the level (close), the market does not agree that price belongs there. Multiple failed wicks at the same level actually increase short confidence.

## Key Points
- **Core rule: candle CLOSE required for BOS.** Wick through level = liquidation only, not confirmation
- Market explanation: wick = price swept the level and found sellers; close back inside = sellers won; market doesn't accept that price
- Multiple wicks at same level (failed BOS attempts) = increasing confidence that level will NOT break → strengthens reversal thesis
- **"Breaker block"** introduced: a bullish OB that has been broken through (mitigated from above) — now acts as overhead resistance / supply area. If price returns to a broken OB zone, it will likely reject from there.
- Candle closure applies to all timeframes — daily, 15m, 1m
- Pattern: price tries to break high (bullish wick), fails to close above → next candle bearish → next also fails to close above → growing certainty that low is being targeted

## Trading Rules / Setups Described
1. **BOS Confirmation Rule:** For any BOS (external or internal), price must CLOSE above/below the swing level. A wick is a liquidation event only. Do NOT count wick-through as a BOS.
2. **Failed Wick Pattern (Short Entry):** Price makes multiple attempts to break above a swing high with wicks, but always closes back below. Each failure increases probability that the low is being targeted. Wait for: any of these wick-failure candles + LTF CHoCH bearish → short entry.
3. **Breaker Block Usage:** A bullish OB that has been subsequently traded through (from below to above — price passed through it) is now a "breaker block." When price returns to test it from above, the old bullish OB acts as supply. Can enter short from breaker level; stop above breaker high; target next structural low.
4. **Direction Determination:** After a confirmed close-above BOS (not a wick), price pulls back. When price finds support at the old swing level and continues with a bullish candle, bias confirmed bullish. Use this for entry zones.
