## Summary
Demonstrates the core LK strategy using the two concepts he considers most fundamental: directional bias (market structure) and order blocks. Notable for its emphasis on wicks as manipulation signals (not BOS), the explicit description of manipulation before OB creation, and a complete live trade breakdown at the London session open. Also establishes LK's primary timeframe is 15m for market analysis.

## Key Points
- **15m is LK's primary analysis timeframe** — all structure, OB, and bias work done here
- **Wick above a high then close back inside range = manipulation signal** (not a BOS). Many traders try to sell this level but it's a trap.
- **OB structure:** Consolidation (range) → manipulation (fake break of a level to induce retail) → aggressive expansion. The consolidation before the expansion = OB zone.
- **Manipulation within OB formation:** Institutions may buy through an internal high to induce retail buyers, then dump — the induced buyers become liquidity for the sell. This confirms the OB.
- **15m structure + OB workflow:** (1) read 15m for directional bias, (2) find 15m OB aligned with bias, (3) await price return to OB, (4) wait for 1m structure shift as confirmation, (5) enter.
- **London session focus:** Primary trading window. Pre-session analysis 30–45 minutes before open (1:15–1:45 a.m. EST).
- **Valid pullback criteria:** Wick does not close below the low = NOT a structural break. Require candle close beyond the level.

## Trading Rules / Setups Described
1. 30–45 minutes before London opens, do 15m analysis: identify current bias (BOS and CHoCH).
2. Find the most recent relevant OB (range + aggressive expansion aligned with bias, that has broken a structural level).
3. Mark the OB zone (the consolidation range before the expansion).
4. As London opens, wait for price to enter the OB zone.
5. On 1m: watch for structure to shift in direction of trade.
6. Enter from 1m/5m level. Stop below OB low. Target: opposing swing high (BSL for longs).
