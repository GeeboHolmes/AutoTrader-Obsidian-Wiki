## Summary
Five "golden keys" to mastering market structure (January 2024), culminating in a full live-trade strategy walkthrough on GBP/USD. Primary new concept: OTE (Optimal Trade Entry) — the 0.618 and 0.786 Fibonacci levels within the discount/premium zone that provide statistically higher reaction probability. Explicitly instructs traders to configure Fibonacci tool to show only 0, 0.5, 0.618, and 0.786 levels.

## Key Points
- **Golden Key 1:** Basic market structure — HH/HL bullish, LH/LL bearish, box method for swing identification.
- **Golden Key 2:** 3-candle pullback rule — the swing high is the highest point inside the range that has at least three candles closing in the opposing direction within the price leg.
- **Golden Key 3:** Practical application of the pullback rule to live charts — confirms box method.
- **Golden Key 4 — Premium/Discount + OTE:** The premium/discount 50% split defines entry quality. The 0.618 and 0.786 levels within the discount (for longs) or premium (for shorts) are the OTE — "Optimal Trade Entry." Price consistently reacts from these levels. Fibonacci settings: 0, 0.5 (equilibrium), 0.618 (OTE 1), 0.786 (OTE 2).
- **Golden Key 5 — Fractal Market Structure:** The same rules apply at every timeframe simultaneously. A 4H bullish structure contains a 15m pullback structure which contains a 1m entry structure. Reading all three fractals and identifying their alignment is the core of multi-timeframe analysis.
- **Probability insight:** Price sometimes skips the 50% entirely and goes straight to 61.8 or 78.6 — when this happens, it is a higher-conviction move. A pull to the 50% is "good"; a pull to 78.6 is "excellent."

## Trading Rules / Setups Described
1. H4 directional bias established via swing structure.
2. When H4 BOS occurs: draw Fibonacci from leg low to high. Mark 50%, 61.8%, and 78.6%.
3. 15m CHoCH in the direction of H4 bias = pullback confirmation.
4. Price enters 50–78.6% zone = entry-eligible area.
5. 1m shift in trade direction from inside OTE zone = entry trigger.
6. Stop: below the OTE zone low (for longs). TP: next structural high.

## Contradictions / Open Questions
- OTE levels (0.618 and 0.786): this is a widespread phenomenon whose theoretical basis is unclear. LK explicitly states "why that is I have no idea but it does work." The fact that price reacts at these levels may be self-fulfilling (many traders use them) or may reflect genuine institutional order placement patterns.
