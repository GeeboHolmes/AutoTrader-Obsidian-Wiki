## Summary
One of LK's earliest complete strategy walkthroughs (July 2023), presenting a five-step framework: (1) directional bias from structure, (2) order blocks / supply-demand zones, (3) liquidity, (4) multi-timeframe analysis, (5) entry, management, and targets. This is a foundational strategy articulation — the same framework used in all subsequent LK strategy videos but stated in cleaner five-step form here.

Key additions beyond the May 2023 course: explicit discussion of the statistical probability that trend continues (70–90% of the time price continues in trend direction; 10–30% is a CHoCH), and an early articulation of liquidity as the algorithm's primary objective ("the market has one job: seek liquidity").

Supply and demand explanation uses an extended economics analogy (apples/buyers/sellers) to explain why price moves to levels — more detailed foundational logic than later videos.

## Key Points
- Five-step strategy: Bias → OB → Liquidity → Multi-TF → Entry/Management.
- 70–90% probability that structure continues in trend direction; CHoCH is only 10–30% of the time.
- Structural high is the highest pullback point inside the price leg before the next BOS — not just any internal high.
- Pullback rule: 3 candles in the opposing direction (three bearish candles each closing below the previous for a bearish pullback).
- OB = "final sell to buy" (demand) or "final buy to sell" (supply) candle with imbalance (FVG).
- Imbalance = when candle 1 and candle 3 don't touch — this is the essential OB qualifier (July 2023 formulation).
- Liquidity = where orders and stop losses cluster; the algorithm's job is to seek and collect these.
- Two types of liquidity: entries (orders being opened) and stop losses (positions being forcibly closed).
- "The market operates as an algorithm — its job is to gravitate toward pockets of liquidity and collect the orders."

## Trading Rules Stated
1. Establish directional bias: identify the most recent BOS and the directional swing structure.
2. Structural high/low = the furthest pullback point within the price leg before the next break — not internal noise.
3. Pullback confirmed by 3 consecutive candles each closing further in the pullback direction.
4. If a candle breaks a swing with a wick only, that is a liquidation — not a structural break.
5. OB = the candle (or final candle in a cluster) before the impulse that leaves imbalance (FVG).
6. Mark unmitigated OBs: if price has already traded through the zone, the OB is mitigated and no longer valid.
7. Identify liquidity: where are the stop clusters? Trade into OBs that have both liquidity taken and liquidity to target.
8. Drop to lower timeframe when price enters the OB; wait for lower TF CHoCH as entry confirmation.
9. Stop above OB (for short) or below OB (for long). Target: next liquidity level / swing low or high.
