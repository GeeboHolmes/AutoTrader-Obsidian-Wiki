## Summary
LK presents a 5-step strategy built around the "Range Liquidation Initiation" (RLI) framework — an institutional mechanics model explaining why OBs form and why price returns to them. The source is one of LK's most educational: explains why ranges occur (institutional accumulation), why liquidations happen (inducement to collect opposing liquidity before the real move), and why the initiation phase always revisits the range. Also introduces DXY (Dollar Index) as a cross-reference confirmation tool.

## Key Points
- Named concept: **Range Liquidation Initiation (RLI)** — three-phase institutional model
  - **Range:** institutional accumulation phase. Price consolidates while institutions build position. Active traders create range; institutions absorb orders.
  - **Liquidation:** inducement/stop-hunt. Institutions temporarily allow price to move opposite direction, collecting opposing liquidity before the real move.
  - **Initiation:** the expansion phase. Institutions initiate the real move. Price returns to the range (OB level) on the way out.
- DXY cross-reference: when EUR/USD is near a stop-loss level, check DXY; if DXY shows a contradicting FVG holding bullish, EUR/USD will hold its level
- 5 steps total (Steps 1-2 fully extracted, Steps 3-5 implied from context):
  - Step 1: Market structure / institutional bias (15m external structure)
  - Step 2: Identify RLI setup — find the Range (consolidation zone), anticipate the Liquidation (inducement), wait for Initiation confirmation
  - Step 3-5: Asia session filter, LTF CHoCH, OB entry (inferred from context — same as base model)
- $100,000 in 90 days claimed; inner circle members replicate trades

## Trading Rules / Setups Described
1. **Step 1 — Institutional Bias:** 15m external structure (box method). Bearish = LH/LL. Identify current swing high and swing low.
2. **Step 2 — RLI Pattern:** Identify consolidation (Range) — a narrow price zone where price is not trending. This is institutional accumulation. Identify the Liquidation — the fake move out of the range that runs stop-losses (often a wick or brief breakout that reverses). Identify the Initiation — the expansion away from the OB that formed during the range/liquidation. Enter at the OB (the area of the range/liquidation) when price returns during the initiation pullback.
3. **Steps 3-5 (inferred):** Asia session filter (sweep of Asia high/low). LTF CHoCH on 1m. Entry at 5m OB/FVG. Target: next structural low/high. Stop: above session high (for shorts).
