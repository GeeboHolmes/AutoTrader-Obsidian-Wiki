## Summary
LK's May 2025 comprehensive SMC guide structured around three core lessons: liquidity, supply & demand (OBs + FVGs), and market structure. Presents the KISS (Keep It Stupid Simple) principle as the antidote to analysis paralysis. The video's strongest contribution is the detailed explanation of WHY institutions need retail liquidity — specifically the mechanics of how a $500M position must be assembled by absorbing counterparty sell orders — which contextualizes why price manipulates retail levels before moving.

## Key Points
- **Liquidity = fuel, not just levels.** Three forms: entries (traders executing), stop-losses (positions closing), take-profits (positions closing). All three add liquidity to the market at identifiable price levels.
- **$500M position mechanics:** An institution buying $500M cannot execute it as a single order — it would move price against itself. It must accumulate by absorbing sell-side liquidity across a range. When sellers are exhausted, the range breaks and price moves aggressively. This is the "ranging = accumulation" model.
- **SMC vs pattern recognition:** Most SMC traders fail because they spot patterns (equal highs, order blocks) without understanding context (why is this level significant? who is positioned here?). Context-based analysis > pattern recognition.
- **OB definition:** Last point of smart money accumulation/distribution before a significant move. Often disguised as consolidation or indecision candles. Price returns to rebalance remaining unfilled orders.
- **FVG definition:** The imbalance between candle 1 and candle 3 created when price moves so aggressively that no transactions occurred in the gap zone. OB = the base; FVG = the trail/confirmation. Together = highest probability.
- **Session liquidity:** Asia high/low, London high/low, daily high/low carry the most significant liquidity clusters. LK's preferred session windows: London (2am–10am EST area), New York AM.
- **Entry model (cheat code):** 15m bullish structure → demand zone → 1m bearish pullback enters zone → 1m CHoCH = entry.

## Trading Rules / Setups Described
1. Identify bias from 15m (or 4H) external structure.
2. Identify all forms of liquidity in the current range: old highs/lows, equal highs/lows, trend lines, session levels (Asia H/L, London H/L, daily H/L).
3. Locate the demand zone: the area of consolidation/accumulation immediately before the last aggressive bullish expansion.
4. Confirm that the demand zone has: (a) proximity to the OB candle cluster, (b) associated FVG (candle 1 high < candle 3 low).
5. Wait for price to trade into the demand zone. Observe 1m structure (will be bearish during pullback).
6. When 1m bearish structure shifts bullish (1m CHoCH — high breaks), this confirms institutions are absorbing sell orders and reversing.
7. Enter long. Stop below 1m swing low. Target: 15m swing high.
