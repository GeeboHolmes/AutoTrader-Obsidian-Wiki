## Summary
Comprehensive liquidity masterclass explaining what liquidity actually is, why traders get it wrong, and how to use it correctly as part of a contextual system. The key insight is that liquidity is worthless without market structure context — knowing where liquidity is doesn't tell you when or whether price will take it. The video introduces the "Smart Money Manipulation Model" as LK's complete framework for using liquidity.

## Key Points
- **Liquidity = money = entries, stop losses, and take profits** of other market participants
- The market is a "psychological" system — retail traders act predictably, making their stop locations identifiable
- **Common retail liquidity sources:** Trendlines (stops below ascending trendlines), equal highs/lows (double tops/bottoms), support/resistance levels, break-and-retest entries
- **"Dumb money map":** Identify all the places retail traders would put entries, stops, and TPs — those are the liquidity pockets
- **Most important rule:** Context (= market structure) must come BEFORE liquidity analysis. Liquidity without context = wrong trades
- **Smart Money Manipulation Model** (LK's 5-step system): bias → area of interest → manipulation → initiation → pullback entry

## Trading Rules / Setups Described
**Smart Money Manipulation Model (5 steps):**
1. **Directional bias:** Market structure on 15m. Determine bullish or bearish. Identify current swing range (swing low to swing high).
2. **Area of interest:** Within the swing range, identify a premium/discount zone (Fibonacci 61.8–78.6%) plus an OB or demand/supply zone.
3. **Manipulation phase:** Price sweeps the retail liquidity levels (trendlines, equal highs/lows, stops). Watch for all the retail patterns to get swept — this is the setup forming.
4. **Initiation:** Aggressive displacement move (large candles) in the true direction after the sweep. Smart money has now filled their orders.
5. **Pullback (mitigation):** Price pulls back into the OB/FVG zone from the initiation move. This is the entry. Wait for LTF confirmation. Stop below the low of the initiation. Target: opposing liquidity.

**How to identify liquidity levels:**
- Draw trendlines under swing lows (ascending) → liquidity below
- Mark equal highs and equal lows → clusters of stops at those levels
- Mark previous day high/low, session highs/lows (Asia, London) → major liquidity
- Mark double tops/bottoms (retail S/R levels) → liquidity above/below them
- Only use liquidity that is INSIDE the current swing range and aligned with bias
