## Summary
LK argues that discipline-based approaches to trading improvement are a lie — motivation is temporary and reliance on it guarantees failure. The real mechanism for consistent profitability is systems and identity, not willpower. LK reveals he has ADHD, which made discipline-based approaches particularly ineffective for him, and that the breakthrough came from designing systems that work WITH his psychology rather than fighting it.

No new technical trading content. Relevant to automated trading bot design: the entire concept of removing human psychology from trading is validated here — a bot IS the system.

## Key Points
- "Discipline trap": trying to use willpower to trade consistently is unsustainable because motivation is borrowed energy, not permanent change.
- New year motivation creates illusion of transformation; environment and decision-making are unchanged.
- LK has ADHD: willpower-based systems failed for him repeatedly.
- Breakthrough: systems that remove the need for discipline. Environment design over willpower.
- "High motivation simply just creates the illusion of transformation. It feels like growth, but it's actually just borrowed energy."
- Sustainable profitability = identity shift + systems design, not more motivation.
- Covered topics: discipline lie → discipline trap → personal ADHD struggle → breakthrough → the system.
