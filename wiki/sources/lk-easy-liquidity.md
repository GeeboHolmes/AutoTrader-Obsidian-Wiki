## Summary
LK presents a 5-part trading model built around the premise that smart money manipulates predictable liquidity pools (stop-loss clusters) before reversing. The video bridges institutional mechanics with a concrete daily trading process: identify key liquidity levels before each session, wait for a sweep of the opposing level, then enter from the OB when the 1-minute structure confirms the reversal.

The strategy is framed around London session execution. LK's game plan is always binary: if buy-side liquidity runs first, trade short; if sell-side runs first, trade long. There is no directional bias established beforehand — the session's first sweep determines which side to trade. This distinguishes it from most LK strategies that pre-establish HTF bias.

The key metric stated: ~49–51% win rate (approximately a "coin flip"), compensated by risk-reward of roughly 1:4 targeting the opposite liquidity extreme (e.g. Asia high after sweeping Asia low).

## Key Points
- Smart money needs to match large orders: they manipulate price to sweep existing stop clusters before moving in their intended direction.
- Every session creates fresh liquidity: Asia high/low, PDL/PDH, equal highs/lows.
- The trade model is purely reactive — wait for price to reveal its hand by sweeping one side, then trade the reversal.
- Liquidity sweep ≠ breakout: a wick through a level with a close back inside = sweep (reversal signal). A close beyond = continuation.
- 1-minute CHoCH after the sweep is the mandatory entry confirmation.
- Entry is from the 5-minute sell-to-buy (or buy-to-sell) OB that caused the CHoCH on 1m.
- Target: Asia session high (after sweeping Asia low) — always the opposite liquidity extreme.
- Win rate ~49–51%. RR ~1:4. Profitable via expectancy.
- Session focus: London (green box). NY secondary.
- Key insight: "If you're buying before the liquidity is taken, you ARE the liquidity."

## Trading Rules / Setups Described

**5-Part Structure:**
1. **Smart money manipulation model** — understand WHY they sweep before moving (need to fill large orders, create opposite-side liquidity).
2. **Key liquidity levels** — mark: Previous Day High/Low, Asia session High/Low, Equal Highs/Equal Lows, visible resistance clusters.
3. **Trade model** — on 15m: identify which side gets swept first (BSL or SSL). After sweep, wait for 15m to show order flow shift.
4. **Entry confirmation** — drop to 1m when price reaches key level. Wait for 1m LH/LL → HL/HH shift (CHoCH). Identify the 5m OB (sell before buy / buy before sell) that caused the CHoCH. Enter from that OB.
5. **Trade execution** — stop loss below the sweep low (or tighter: below the 5m OB). Target = opposite session liquidity extreme (Asia high or PDH).

**Entry Decision Framework:**
- Come into London session. Identify Asia High and PDL on 15m.
- If London runs Asia High (BSL) first → look for short entry.
- If London runs Asia Low/PDL (SSL) first → look for long entry.
- Wait for the run + 1m CHoCH before taking any trade.

## Contradictions / Open Questions
- States ~49–51% win rate — lower than most LK strategies (others claim 33–40%). Likely because this strategy has no HTF bias filter: it takes whatever side gets swept first regardless of weekly/daily structure.
- How to resolve conflict when both Asia High and PDL are swept within the same London session? LK does not address this.
- No mention of premium/discount filter — entry could be in unfavorable zone. This is a potential weakness vs other LK strategies.
