## Summary
Six-part advanced market structure course (July 2023). Introduces LK's "order flow" terminology for internal structure and the "order flow realignment" entry model — the earliest known statement of what later became the "realignment" concept central to all LK strategies. The course establishes three levels of structure: swing structure (external), internal structure, and order flow (LTF). Covers equal highs/lows as liquidity pools and the mechanics of why equal highs/lows get swept before price continues.

## Key Points
- **Order flow** = LK's term for the internal structure within a swing range. Equivalent to what other educators call "LTF internal structure." When external structure is bullish and internal (order flow) is bearish, the market is in a pullback.
- **Order flow realignment** = when bearish order flow (inside a bullish swing) shifts bullish again — this is the LTF CHoCH that signals the pullback is complete and the bullish leg continues. This is the entry trigger.
- **Three types of structure hierarchy:**
  1. *Swing structure* — external high/low defining the current trading range.
  2. *Internal structure* — all price action inside the swing range.
  3. *Order flow* — the present-moment directional bias of the internal structure.
- **Equal highs/lows mechanism:** When price creates equal highs on both sides, there is a pool of liquidity (buy/sell stops) on each side. Price will run one side first, then the other. The side that gets swept first funds the move to the other side.
- **Pre-dates realignment concept:** The July 2023 formulation ("order flow realignment") is the earliest instance of LK's realignment entry model, later formalised in [[sources/lk-advanced-smc-2023]] (June 2023 — earlier by one month).

## Trading Rules / Setups Described
1. **Swing structure setup:** Identify external swing high/low (the current trading range).
2. **Order flow read:** Map internal structure within the range. Identify whether order flow is aligned with or opposed to the swing structure.
3. **Pullback identification:** When swing structure is bullish and order flow makes a series of LH/LL internally — this is a pullback, not a reversal.
4. **Entry trigger (order flow realignment):** When bearish order flow makes a higher high internally (breaks above the most recent internal high) — this is the order flow realignment. The area of demand that caused this shift is the OB entry zone.
5. **Target:** External structural high (the swing high that defined the trading range).

## Contradictions / Open Questions
