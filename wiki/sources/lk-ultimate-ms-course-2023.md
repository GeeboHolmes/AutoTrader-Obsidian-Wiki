## Summary
LK's earliest dedicated market structure course (May 2023), walking from beginner HH/HL/LH/LL basics all the way to multi-timeframe analysis. The video introduces the three-step progression: simple structure → multi-timeframe structure → advanced application. It is the foundational statement of LK's mechanical market structure reading system.

LK establishes that structure is defined by swing points — the external points where price reverses. He emphasises the close-only rule for BOS (wicks do not count) and the 3-candle pullback rule as a mechanical filter. The multi-timeframe section shows how the 15m and 4H interact: when the 4H is bearish but the 15m is bullish, price is in a pullback phase; when the 15m realigns bearish, the 4H will take out its swing low.

This video predates most of the more refined formulations later in the series, but the core mechanics are already fully formed here.

## Key Points
- Structure is the sequence of HH/HL (bullish) or LH/LL (bearish) formed by swing points.
- Swing points are where price *reverses* — the external extremes of each price leg.
- BOS requires a candle close beyond the previous swing point; wicks do not count — they are "liquidations."
- A pullback is validated by 3 consecutive candles each closing below (bearish) or above (bullish) the previous one.
- Internal structure (mini highs/lows inside the swing range) is noise; only external swing points count for bias.
- When 15m is bullish inside a 4H bearish trend → 4H pullback phase; when 15m shifts bearish → 4H will take out the swing low.
- Supply and demand zones are created when an imbalance exists (candle 1 and candle 3 don't overlap) — this is an early formulation of the FVG rule.

## Trading Rules Stated
1. Mark swing highs and lows from external reversals only (not internal structure).
2. BOS is confirmed when price breaks AND closes beyond the previous swing point. Wicks alone = liquidation, not BOS.
3. A structural pullback requires 3 consecutive candles that each close further than the prior candle in the pullback direction.
4. Trade in the direction of the higher timeframe (4H) structure; the 15m tells you when the pullback is done.
5. When the 15m shifts back to align with the 4H trend direction → entry signal (order flow realignment).
6. Supply zones (for shorts) = last bullish candle before bearish impulse that leaves an imbalance.
7. Demand zones (for longs) = last bearish candle before bullish impulse that leaves an imbalance.
8. Look for imbalance (gap between candle 1 and candle 3) to qualify a supply/demand zone.
