## Summary
A live trade walkthrough showing a 28R trade from London session (August 2023). LK demonstrates the complete 15m → 1m entry model in practice. This video emphasises the "past → present → future" narrative framing of structure reading, and introduces a key practical refinement: when price ranges are large, drop to 3m or 5m (not just 1m) to identify OBs and time the entry more cleanly.

The video showcases the standard entry model: 15m bearish swing structure identified → price pulls into OB/supply → 1m shifts bearish (OFR) → entry at supply zone → target is 15m swing low. The 28R came from the unusually large price range of the leg.

Key practical point: high R multiples are mechanical outputs of the structural range — no discretion involved. The system's RR is determined by the distance between entry zone and structural target, not by prediction.

## Key Points
- "To understand where we are right now we have to understand where we've come from" — always read structure as a narrative: past → present → future.
- Three-candle pullback rule: requires each of the 3 candles to close further in the pullback direction (verified one by one on chart).
- Wick through a high without close = liquidation, not BOS. Must confirm with body close.
- High R multiples (28R) are mechanical — they result from large structural ranges, not prediction.
- When price ranges are large and the 1m is too messy, refine to 3m or 5m to identify cleaner OBs.
- "Decisional area" = the OB/supply zone that caused the structural break (CHoCH/BOS). This is the primary POI.
- "Origin" = the unmitigated OB from further back in the leg; secondary POI if price overshoots the decisional.
- Sessions: LK trades 8am–12pm London time (11am–3pm Dubai time). Position left open once at break even.
- Partial profits: move to break even when price takes out the structural swing low (intermediate target).

## Trading Rules Stated
1. Read structure left to right: identify where structure was, where it is now, where it is going.
2. For every swing point: apply 3-candle pullback rule — if a move doesn't have 3 consecutive closing candles in the pullback direction, it is part of the same price leg.
3. BOS = break AND close beyond the swing point. Liquidation = wick beyond without close.
4. Primary POI = "decisional area" (the OB that caused the last structural break).
5. Secondary POI = "origin" (unmitigated higher OB in the same leg).
6. Drop to 1m (or 3m/5m if messy) when price enters POI. Wait for 1m OFR (shift from bullish to bearish inside the supply zone).
7. Stop = above the high of the POI plus buffer (3.5 pips stated as example).
8. Initial target = 15m structural swing low (first weak low in trend).
9. Move to break even when price breaks the first intermediate swing low.
10. Trade London session window only (personal rule); always out before New York open unless break even secured.
