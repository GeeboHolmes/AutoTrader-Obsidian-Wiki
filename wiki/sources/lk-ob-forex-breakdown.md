## Summary
LK reveals three "secrets" that explain why most traders fail with order blocks. The video is framed around common failure modes rather than a new strategy: (1) not understanding trend context, (2) trading OBs at the wrong time relative to Asia session liquidity, and (3) trading OBs that sit on resting liquidity. The bonus (and most actionable) content is a multi-timeframe confirmation technique that significantly boosts risk-reward without reducing win rate.

LK claims $117,000 in profit across 11 trading days in November, and $6,000 in 40 minutes on the trade shown. These claims are context for why the concepts are high-value.

## Key Points
- **Secret 1 — Context is King:** An OB is only valid if it aligns with the broader market trend (external structure). Trading any OB from an impulsive move without checking the overall directional bias is the primary failure mode.
- **Secret 2 — Right Place, Wrong Time:** OBs that sit inside or near the Asia session range (8pm–midnight EST) should be avoided. Asia liquidity will almost certainly be swept before London opens, meaning any OB inside this range will be penetrated. The rule: get involved *after* Asia liquidity is taken.
- **Secret 3 — The Liquidity Trap:** Never trade an OB that sits directly on resting liquidity (equal lows, key swing lows). When momentum enters that OB and triggers your entry, the same momentum will continue to sweep the resting liquidity below it, stopping you out. The stop loss placement (below the OB) coincides exactly with the liquidity cluster.
- **Bonus — Multi-Timeframe Confirmation:** Drop to 1m when price enters the HTF OB. Wait for 1m CHoCH (lower low → lower high → run of high = bullish shift). Then drop to 5m and identify the 5m OB/FVG that produced that 1m CHoCH. Enter from the 5m OB. This converts a ~1:3.27 entry into a ~1:6 entry by tightening the stop loss.

## Trading Rules / Setups Described
1. Confirm external market structure bias (only trade pro-trend OBs).
2. Do NOT enter OBs that sit inside the Asia session range — wait for London to open and sweep Asia liquidity first.
3. Do NOT enter OBs that sit directly above (bullish) or below (bearish) resting liquidity (equal lows, swing lows). Wait for that liquidity to be swept first, THEN enter the OB below.
4. When price enters your pre-identified OB: drop to 1m. Watch for 1m CHoCH (structure shift in direction of trade).
5. On 1m CHoCH confirmed: go to 5m. Identify the 5m OB/FVG that caused the CHoCH.
6. Enter from 5m OB. Stop loss above the 5m OB high. Target: 15m structural high (or equivalent).
7. Result: stop goes from ~8 pip to ~4 pip. RR goes from ~1:3.27 to ~1:6.

## Contradictions / Open Questions
- "Secret 3 — avoid OBs on liquidity" appears to contradict some of LK's own strategies where he enters at the OB just above or at the liquidity pool. Resolution: the distinction is whether the liquidity has *already been swept* vs *pending to be swept*. After sweep = valid entry; liquidity still sitting = trap.
