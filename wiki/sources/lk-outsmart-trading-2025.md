## Summary
Three rules for outplaying the game of trading. Rule 1: "The Mirror" — the market mirrors your personality. Impatient traders force trades; hard workers overtrade; identity shapes outcomes. Rule 2 and Rule 3 (not fully captured in sampled content) likely continue the theme. Primarily psychology/identity content with no new technical rules.

Key insight: trading rewards patient, process-driven behaviour — the opposite of what "hard work" means in corporate life. Hard workers in trading overtrade, revenge trade, and force setups because they cannot tolerate patience.

## Key Points
- "The Market is a Mirror" — your psychological traits manifest directly in your trading results.
- Impatient traders: force trades, can't wait for setups.
- Hard worker archetype: overtrades, overanalyzes, revenge trades, forces entries — because "hard work" means something different in trading (patience, not activity).
- Trading rewards smart work (patience, process) not hard work (activity, effort).
- "Trading isn't about hard work — it's about smart work. You can work hard in your preparation, but when it comes to actually placing trades — this just requires patience."
- Character archetypes: impatient, hard worker, perfectionist, risk-averse — all have specific manifestations in trading failure.
