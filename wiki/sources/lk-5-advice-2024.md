## Summary
A Q&A format video where LK answers community questions. Technical content includes an unusually precise explanation of the 3-candle pullback rule — specifically clarifying that the THREE candles must each consecutively close beyond the prior candle. A candle that only partially closes below (not below the previous candle) does not count. This is the most detailed mechanical clarification of this rule in the transcript archive.

Also discusses: FVGs as targets (not entries) in LK's system, trading style choice based on personality, and partial exits at break-even for overnight positions.

## Key Points
- **3-candle pullback rule clarification (critical):** The three candles must EACH close below the previous one sequentially. If candle 2 does not close below candle 1, it doesn't count as a qualifying candle — you restart the count.
- Example: bearish candle 1 → bearish candle 2 that does NOT close below candle 1 → bearish candle 3 that closes below candle 1 but not 2. This gives you only TWO qualifying candles, not three. Not a pullback.
- FVGs in LK's system: used as "liquidity voids" / potential targets for price to return to, NOT as entry zones or POIs. OBs are the entry tool.
- Swing vs intraday trading: personality-dependent. LK prefers intraday; holding overnight positions creates psychological strain unless partials/break-even are secured first.
- Minimum viable position overnight: take 80–90% off at partials, leave 10–20% with stops at break even; this removes psychological investment.

## Trading Rules Stated
1. 3-candle pullback: candle 2 must close beyond candle 1, AND candle 3 must close beyond candle 2. Non-consecutive closes reset the count.
2. FVGs = targets/liquidity voids. Do NOT enter from FVGs — enter from OBs only.
3. Only hold positions overnight if majority of position is already off and stop is at break even.
