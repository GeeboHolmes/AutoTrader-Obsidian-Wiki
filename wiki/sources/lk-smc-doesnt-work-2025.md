## Summary
LK argues that SMC traders fail not because of bad concepts but because they use SMC tools as **pattern recognition** rather than **contextual analysis**. The "one thing" = treating OBs and FVGs as patterns to spot rather than understanding the institutional narrative behind them. Presents the core 4-step framework as a counterexample of simplicity over complexity.

## Key Concepts Extracted

### Context Over Pattern Recognition (Core Thesis)
- Most SMC traders fail because they look for patterns (OB shape, FVG shape) rather than understanding *why* those patterns form.
- The goal of SMC is to understand institutional order flow and follow it — not to spot visual patterns.
- "The market is a game of supply and demand. Your job is to understand where the big players are and follow them."
- Fewer concepts mastered deeply = better than many concepts used shallowly.

### 4-Step Framework (Simple Formulation)
The clearest and most condensed statement of the standard LK workflow:
1. **15m directional bias** — swing structure only: are we making HH/HL or LH/LL? That's the trade direction.
2. **Areas of interest** — identify OBs and FVGs in the direction of bias; the OB is where the aggressive impulsive candle originated.
3. **Liquidity confirmation** — a liquidity level must have been swept before entering the OB. The OB whose liquidity is *unswept* below it is invalid (price will continue through to take that liquidity first).
4. **1m CHoCH confirmation** — drop to 1m when price enters OB zone; wait for 1m structure to flip in trade direction before entering. "Don't guess, confirm."

### OB Width Trade-Off
- Wider OB zone: easier to see, easier to get filled, but more noise — price may enter multiple times before a real move.
- Narrower/refined OB: might miss entry if price only touches the extreme; but tighter stop = better RR.
- No "right or wrong" — use context to decide. The zone represents institutional accumulation; the exact boundary is less important than the area.

### Absorption Mechanic (OB Zone Mechanics)
How to read what's happening inside an OB zone:
- When price enters the OB, sellers (or buyers for bearish) push back aggressively.
- **Ranging inside the zone** = absorption phase. The opposing side (buyers in a bullish OB) is absorbing sell orders one-for-one.
- When buyers fully absorb sellers' orders, price exits the range and pushes higher → this is the 1m CHoCH signal.
- "Think of it like a war — supply puts in their orders, buyers absorb them. When buyers win, they push price out."
- **Python implication:** ranging + tight candle bodies inside OB zone → absorption. CHoCH following the range = confirmation.

### What SMC Traders Get Wrong
- Over-complication: learning 100 concepts instead of mastering 4 steps.
- Pattern recognition: marking OBs mechanically without understanding the institutional context behind them.
- Blind entries: entering at OBs without waiting for market confirmation (1m CHoCH).
- Ignoring liquidity: not checking whether resting liquidity below the OB will pull price through before the reaction.

## Trading Rules Stated
1. Use 15m for bias (swing structure only — HH/HL or LH/LL).
2. Identify demand zone (OB or FVG) in direction of bias.
3. Check for liquidity context: there must be a swept liquidity level confirming institutional activity near the OB. If unswept liquidity sits below the OB, it is likely to be taken out first.
4. Enter on 1m CHoCH inside the OB zone. Stop below the zone (or below the 1m swing low formed in the zone). Target: next structural high.
5. Use M3 or M5 OB inside the zone for refined entry (same confirmation method, smaller timeframe).
