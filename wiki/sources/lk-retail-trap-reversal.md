## Summary
LK presents a retail trap reversal strategy built around identifying where retail traders place stops — equal highs, resistance zones, trendlines, round numbers, prior session highs/lows — then waiting for the institutional sweep of those levels before entering in the opposite direction. The $8,000 trade is the live example.

The strategy formalises what LK calls the "liquidity trap": retail traders accumulate stops above visible resistance and below visible support. Institutions target those stops, spike through the level, collect the liquidity, then reverse. The strategy requires identifying the trap zone first, then waiting patiently for the sweep and LTF confirmation before entering.

## Key Points
- $8,000 live trade example (3 hours to hit target)
- Core thesis: retail stops cluster at visible levels; institutions target those clusters
- Retail liquidity clusters: equal highs/lows, trendlines, S/R lines, round numbers, prior session extremes
- 4-step process: identify cluster → wait for sweep → LTF CHoCH → OB/FVG entry
- Stop: above the wick of the sweep candle (outside the trap zone)
- Target: 1:3 RR minimum; nearest structural support/resistance
- Key distinction: this is NOT a trend-following model — it is a reversal model triggered by manipulation

## Trading Rules / Setups Described
1. **Step 1 — Identify Retail Liquidity Cluster:** On 15m or 1H, find a zone where retail traders have concentrated stops. Signals: equal highs (two or more highs at the same price), trendline with multiple touches, clear resistance/support level, prior session high/low, round number. Mark the zone as a pending liquidity pool.
2. **Step 2 — Wait for Manipulation (Sweep):** Do nothing until London or NY session runs THROUGH the liquidity cluster. Price must breach the level, triggering retail stops. The candle that sweeps will often be a wick (spike through and back) or a brief breakout that fails to hold.
3. **Step 3 — LTF CHoCH Confirmation:** After sweep, drop to 1m. Wait for 1m market structure to shift in the opposite direction (CHoCH). This confirms institutions have collected liquidity and are now reversing. Do not enter before CHoCH.
4. **Step 4 — Entry at OB/FVG + Target 1:3:** Enter at 1m or 5m OB/FVG in the reversal direction. Stop above the wick of the sweep candle (above the manipulation high for shorts / below the manipulation low for longs). Target: 1:3 RR from entry to the next significant structural level.

## Contradictions / Open Questions

- 1:3 RR target is lower than other LK models (which use 1:5 for A+ setups). This may reflect the shorter swing distance inherent in trap reversals.
