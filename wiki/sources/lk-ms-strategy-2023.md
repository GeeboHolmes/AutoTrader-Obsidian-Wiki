## Summary
LK's August 2023 full breakdown of market structure, introducing the explicit three-type structure framework: (1) Swing Structure, (2) Order Flow, and (3) Order Flow Realignment (OFR). This is the clearest early statement of the realignment concept and the labeling "OFR" before it became the standard shorthand.

This video formally introduces the color-coded three-layer model: swing structure (external reversals, red), order flow (internal present-moment direction, purple), and order flow realignment (the moment internal aligns with external, green). The OFR is the precise entry signal — it tells you before price gets to its target that the target is about to be reached.

Premium/discount model is presented using a simple Fibonacci overlay (0–0.5–1) and the rule that pullbacks retrace to at least 50% of the price leg approximately 70% of the time.

## Key Points
- Three types of structure: Swing (external reversals) → Order Flow (internal present moment) → OFR (internal realigns with external).
- Swing structure = external swing points that cause price reversal; defines the trading range.
- Order flow = present-moment directional flow inside the swing range; always opposite to swing during pullback.
- Order flow realignment (OFR) = when internal structure shifts to align with the swing direction. This is the entry signal.
- OFR gives you a "value discrepancy" — you know where price is heading BEFORE it gets there.
- Premium/discount: split the price leg at 50% with a Fibonacci. Sell in premium (top half), buy in discount (bottom half).
- Price pulls back to at least 50% approximately 70% of the time after a BOS.
- "Change of character is completely unpredictable" — you cannot predict WHEN it will happen, only react to it. 80% of the time it does NOT happen (the trend continues).
- When order flow is bullish (pullback) inside a bearish swing, you are NOT confused — you are watching the pullback phase. Wait for OFR.

## Trading Rules Stated
1. Identify swing structure (HTF bias): mark the external HH/HL or LH/LL that define the current trading range.
2. Inside the trading range, the internal structure (order flow) will temporarily move counter-swing. This is normal — it is the pullback.
3. When order flow shifts back to align with swing structure (OFR = internal CHoCH in the swing direction), this is the entry signal.
4. The OFR confirms: the next swing target (structural low for bearish, structural high for bullish) is now imminent.
5. Draw Fibonacci from the most recent swing high to swing low. Pullbacks into the premium zone (0–0.5 range for bearish) are the highest probability entry zones.
6. 70% of the time, price will pull back to at least the 50% level. Use this as a minimum expectation for entries.
7. Drop to 1m when price enters the OB/supply zone to find the OFR (1m internal CHoCH in trade direction).
8. Entry is at the OFR level; stop above/below the 1m swing that caused the OFR.
