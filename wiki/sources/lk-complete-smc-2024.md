## Summary
LK presents the "complete strategy blueprint" in four parts: directional bias, institutional areas, strategy rules, and live backtest. The key technical contribution is the "box method" for accurate swing low identification — a mechanical, objective way to determine the correct structural reference point after a BOS. This eliminates a common mistake where traders use an internal low instead of the true structural low.

The entry model is the sell-to-buy OB (the last bearish candle before the impulsive bullish move that broke structure), confirmed by 1m CHoCH when price re-enters the zone. Minimum 1:5 RR.

## Key Points
- **Box method for swing low ID:** After a BOS, draw a box from the previous swing low to the swing high. The correct new swing low is the LOWEST point inside that box before price broke out. This is the structural low that is now "protected" and defines the current trend.
- Common mistake: traders use the first internal low after the BOS, not the lowest point before the breakout. This causes them to get stopped out by internal moves that don't invalidate structure.
- **OB definition (sell-to-buy):** The last bearish candle before the aggressive bullish impulse that broke structure. This is where institutional buy orders were placed. When price retraces here, those orders refill.
- 30m/15m used for structural bias (medium timeframe). 1m for entry confirmation.
- **1m entry model:** Drop to 1m when price re-enters the OB zone. Observe bearish 1m structure. Wait for 1m CHoCH (lower low → higher high). The OB at the 1m CHoCH level is the entry.
- Stop loss: below the 1m low (tight). Stop below swing low (wider — use only if stop below OB is too large).
- Target: next structural swing high (or HTF liquidity). Minimum 1:5 RR.
- Key profit expectancy math: 50% WR × 1:5 RR = net 5× risk per 10 trades even. 5 losers = -5R, 5 winners = +15R, net = +10R.

## Trading Rules / Setups Described
**Step 1 — Directional Bias:**
1. Map external swing points on 30m/15m.
2. Draw a box from swing low to swing high. Anything inside is internal — ignore for bias.
3. A BOS occurs when price breaks out of the box. The new swing low is the lowest internal point before the break.
4. If BOS bullish and swing low respected → bias is LONG.

**Step 2 — Institutional Area:**
1. Identify the "sell-to-buy" level — the area where price consolidated/consolidated before the bullish impulse.
2. This is the OB: last bearish candle (or range) before the aggressive expansion.
3. Confirm: there must be a BOS in this price leg; the OB must be inside the active trading range.

**Step 3 — Strategy Blueprint:**
1. HTF structure confirmed bullish → bias = long.
2. Price pulls back toward OB. Do NOT enter immediately.
3. Drop to 1m when price enters OB zone. Observe 1m bearish structure. Wait for 1m CHoCH (higher high breaking previous high).
4. Identify 1m OB (sell-to-buy at the CHoCH level).
5. Enter long from 1m OB. Stop below 1m low. Target next HTF structural high. Min 1:5 RR.
