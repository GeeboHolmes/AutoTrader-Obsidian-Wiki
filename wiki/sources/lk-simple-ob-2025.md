## Summary
LK presents a three-rule OB model designed to eliminate the most common OB trading mistakes and dramatically improve RR. The three rules are progressive: follow the money (direction filter), avoid the traps (entry zone filter), and order flow confirmation (entry timing). The video's central claim is that adding rule 3 alone can transform a 1:2 trade into a 1:9+ trade through tighter entry placement and improved confirmation.

Three OB subtypes are identified (final sell-to-buy, multi-candle, first momentum candle), though all three must still satisfy the same three rules.

## Key Points
- Rule 1 (Follow the Money): Only trade OBs in the direction of **external** structure. A bearish OB inside a bullish external range = counter-trend, will fail. Traders reading internal structure get trapped.
- Rule 2 (Avoid the Traps): OBs that sit at unswept liquidity levels (equal highs/lows, Asia H/L, PDH/PDL, London H/L) will be penetrated. Price will sweep through the OB to collect the liquidity before reacting from the real level below.
- Rule 3 (Order Flow Confirmation): When price enters the OB zone, wait for the 1m timeframe to shift from bearish to bullish (1m CHoCH) before entering. This tightens the stop dramatically and adds confirmation that the OB is holding.
- OB types documented: (1) final sell-to-buy candle, (2) multi-candle consolidation before the expansion, (3) first momentum candle at the beginning of the expansion.
- RR math example: without confirmation, stop = below OB = 1:2 RR. With 1m CHoCH inside OB, stop = below 1m structure shift = 1:9 or 1:10+ RR. Same trade, same direction, 4.5× better return on risk.

## Trading Rules / Setups Described
1. Identify external structure trend (bullish or bearish).
2. Locate a valid OB (sell-to-buy for bullish trend) in the direction of the trend.
3. **Trap check:** Ensure no unswept liquidity (equal lows, Asia/London session low, PDL) sits between current price and the OB. If liquidity is present below the OB, wait for it to be swept first. The OB below the unswept liquidity is likely to be "run through."
4. When price enters the valid OB (trap-free, trend-aligned), drop to 1m.
5. Observe 1m structure: price will be bearish (pullback phase) as it enters the OB.
6. Wait for 1m bearish structure to **break bullish** (1m high breaks → 1m CHoCH bullish).
7. Entry: buy at the level of the 1m structure shift, placing stop below the 1m swing low.
8. Target: next structural high (swing high of the active leg).
