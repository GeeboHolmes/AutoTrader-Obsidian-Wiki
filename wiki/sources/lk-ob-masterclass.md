## Summary
LK's definitive early OB selection framework — 3 rules for identifying high-probability OBs plus a complete step-by-step strategy applied to a live trade. The most important new rule here is Rule 2: an OB must be preceded by a **run on liquidity**. The sell-to-buy (or buy-to-sell) that forms the OB must itself have swept a liquidity level (equal lows, previous swing low) before the aggressive expansion. This run on liquidity is what qualifies the OB as institutional.

The minimum RR stated here is 1:5, with 1m confirmation entry enabling this. Asia session low sweep is explicitly required before any long entry (the OB must be below Asia session low).

## Key Points
- **Rule 1 — Pro-Trend:** OB must align with the external structure trend. Never trade a bearish OB when market is bullish.
- **Rule 2 — Run on Liquidity:** The OB (sell-to-buy level) must have triggered a run on some liquidity before the aggressive expansion. The "Final sell" before the buy must have swept a low (equal lows, internal low). Without liquidity sweep, there is no institutional signature.
- **Rule 3 — BOS in price leg:** The OB must sit inside a structural price leg that produced a BOS. If no BOS was created from this price leg, the OB is weak.
- **FVG required:** "Your order block should always have a fair value gap." Candle 1's high should not touch candle 3's low (for bullish OB). No FVG = no OB.
- **Asia session rule:** If buying, the OB should be below Asia session low — specifically, the OB must be accessed via a sweep of Asia liquidity. London sweeps Asia low → price retraces to OB below Asia low → 1m CHoCH → long entry. Asia high becomes take profit.
- **1:3.3 RR if entered at HTF OB directly.** But minimum is 1:5. Solution: 1m confirmation narrows stop, raising RR to 1:5+.
- **1m entry process:** Price sweeps Asia low → enters OB → 1m structure is bearish → wait for 1m run of liquidity then CHoCH → identify 1m OB at the CHoCH level → enter from 1m OB with tight stop → target Asia high.

## Trading Rules / Setups Described
1. Identify 30m directional bias (external structure: BOS bullish).
2. Asia session opens — mark Asia High and Asia Low.
3. Coming into London: expect Asia Low to be swept (SSL liquidation) if bullish.
4. Pre-identify OB below Asia Low: last sell-to-buy candle that produced a run of liquidity (swept equal lows) and sits inside a BOS-producing structural price leg. OB must have FVG.
5. London sweeps Asia Low. Price enters OB zone.
6. Drop to 1m. Observe bearish structure. Wait for 1m liquidity run (takes out a low) then 1m CHoCH (higher high).
7. Identify 1m sell-to-buy with FVG at the CHoCH level.
8. Enter long from 1m OB. Stop below 1m low. Target Asia High (or next structural high). Min 1:5 RR.

## Contradictions / Open Questions
- Rule 2 (OB must have a run on liquidity before forming) is a stricter requirement than stated in other LK sources. Some sources allow OBs without a prior liquidity sweep if they have a FVG and BOS. This may be a case of LK refining his criteria over time.
- Minimum RR of 1:5 stated here. But later LK strategies (100K strategy) use fixed 1:3. The choice depends on strategy type.
