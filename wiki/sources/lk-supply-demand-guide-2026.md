## Summary
Deep dive into the mechanics of how markets actually work at the order book level, explaining supply and demand from first principles. Explains passive vs aggressive liquidity, how candlesticks are formed by order book dynamics, why slippage happens, and why FVGs represent areas where institutional orders were placed aggressively. Most valuable for understanding the *why* behind OBs and FVGs at a microstructure level.

## Key Points
- **Balance = fair value = range.** When buyers and sellers agree, price ranges (consolidation). When either side overwhelms, price moves aggressively.
- **A FVG = an imbalanced market.** When price moves so aggressively there's no two-sided trading in the gap — that gap must eventually be filled.
- **Market cycles:** Balance (range) → Imbalance (expansion) → new Balance → new Imbalance. This is fractal across all timeframes.
- **Order book mechanics:** Passive liquidity (limit orders sitting in the book) vs aggressive orders (market orders that sweep through the book). FVGs form when aggressive orders overwhelm passive liquidity causing price to skip levels.
- **Slippage explanation:** Occurs when the aggressive order size exceeds available passive liquidity at the target price — price moves to find the next available passive order.
- **Two ways OBs form:** (1) Institutions actively buy fake breakout (to create retail FOMO) then sell into the liquidity. (2) Institutions stop accumulating → buyers push through without resistance → institutions then re-accumulate at the new level.

## Trading Rules / Setups Described
1. Understand market is always cycling: balance (range) → imbalance (expansion).
2. After an aggressive expansion, the origin range (balance area) = OB zone. The gap = FVG.
3. Five truths about supply & demand: (1) context matters (trend alignment), (2) strong zones have confluence (multiple levels), (3) first touch = highest probability, (4) manipulated zones = highest probability (sweep before entry), (5) confirmation required.
4. Enter from the OB/FVG on return, with LTF confirmation.
