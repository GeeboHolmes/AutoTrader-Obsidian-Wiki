## Summary
LK presents a dedicated daily bias system — the HTF layer that sits above all intraday strategies. The thesis: the previous day's candle tells you what today will do; institutional algorithms deliver price via a predictable three-phase pattern (Power of Three: accumulation, manipulation, distribution). This source is the most complete treatment of LK's daily candle reading methodology.

The strategy has three layers: (1) daily candle character read, (2) HTF magnet identification (equal highs/lows, FVGs on higher timeframes that price is drawn to), and (3) Power of Three intraday execution — wait for the manipulation phase (opposite direction) then trade the continuation. The key insight: every daily candle opens, does a fake move (manipulation), then delivers the real move (distribution).

## Key Points
- Previous day candle character determines next day directional bias
- Four patterns for daily candle reading (see rules below)
- HTF magnets: equal highs/lows and FVGs on H4/Daily act as price targets
- Power of Three: accumulation (Asia) → manipulation (London fake) → distribution (NY real move)
- Manipulation phase = liquidity grab on the wrong side before the real move
- Entry: 15m OB/FVG after manipulation phase confirms
- Session timing: wait for manipulation to complete before entering distribution phase

## Trading Rules / Setups Described
1. **Step 1 — Daily Candle Character:** Read the previous day's closed candle against prior structure.
   - **Bearish continuation:** Previous day closed BELOW the prior day's low (PDL). Expect next day to continue down.
   - **Bullish continuation:** Previous day closed ABOVE the prior day's high (PDH). Expect next day to continue up.
   - **Bearish rejection:** Previous day wicked ABOVE PDH (tapped liquidity above) but CLOSED back inside the prior range. Expect next day to drop.
   - **Bullish rejection:** Previous day wicked BELOW PDL (tapped liquidity below) but CLOSED back inside the prior range. Expect next day to rise.
2. **Step 2 — HTF Magnet:** Identify the nearest significant liquidity pool or imbalance on H4 or Daily. Equal highs/lows, unfilled FVGs, and session extremes act as magnets that price is algorithmically drawn toward. This becomes the target for the distribution phase.
3. **Step 3 — Power of Three Entry:** During Asia (8pm–midnight EST): accumulation (range forms). At London open (2am EST): manipulation phase — price moves AGAINST the daily bias to sweep opposing liquidity. This is the trap. Once manipulation completes (LTF CHoCH signals end of fake move), enter at 15m OB/FVG in the direction of the daily bias. Target: HTF magnet identified in Step 2.
