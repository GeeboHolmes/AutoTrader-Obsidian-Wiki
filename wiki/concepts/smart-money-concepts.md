## Definition
Smart Money Concepts (SMC) is a price action methodology based on the premise that large institutional participants ("smart money" — banks, hedge funds, market makers) leave identifiable footprints in price data. By reading these footprints, retail traders can align with institutional order flow rather than trade against it.

## Core Premise
Markets are not random. They are engineered by institutional participants to:
1. **Build liquidity** — induce retail traders into predictable positions (above swing highs, below swing lows).
2. **Raid liquidity** — hunt those positions (stop hunts, liquidity sweeps) to fill large institutional orders.
3. **Deliver price** — move price efficiently to the next area of interest (imbalance fill, order block mitigation).

The retail trader's edge is identifying *where* institutions have placed orders and *when* they are likely to act.

## Key Distinction from Traditional TA
- Traditional TA: support/resistance, indicators, patterns.
- SMC: *why* price moves (liquidity grabs, order delivery) rather than *what* patterns appear.
- SMC practitioners typically use clean charts with no indicators, reading pure price structure.
